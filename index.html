About Me

I am a Senior Lecturer at the School of Data and Sciences at BRAC University and an active Researcher in Machine Learning. My work lies at the intersection of Deep Learning, Signal Processing, and Computer Vision, with a primary focus on ML-based solutions for Healthcare and Diagnostics.

My personality type is ENTP-A, and I have a multidisciplinary academic background (EE, CS). This is probably why I am naturally drawn to exploring novel ideas and the connections between diverse fields. As such, I have a quite wide range of research interests, spanning from Multi-Modal Machine Learning & Deep Learning Architectures, to their applications in Biomedical Imaging, Bioinformatics and even Computational Social Science. My current research revolves around developing deep-learning frameworks that efficiently learn from multiple signal representations simultaneously, leveraging multi-modal fusion based architectures. At BRAC University, I teach basic courses like Circuits & Electronics and also advanced courses like Image Processing, which aligns closely with my own research.

You can explore the different pages on this site to find my detailed research summary, a full list of publications, achievements and my complete teaching portfolio.
